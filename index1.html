Forest Resources

The Philippines once had a vast forest cover, with over 70% of the country's land area forested. However, deforestation has led to a significant decline in forest cover, with only about 24% of the country now forested. The remaining forests are classified as follows:

Natural Forests

These are forests that have not been significantly altered by human activities. They are home to a wide variety of plant and animal species and are important for biodiversity conservation.

Plantation Forests

These are forests that have been planted with trees for timber production or other purposes. They are often less biodiverse than natural forests, but they still provide valuable forest products.

Mangrove Forests

These are forests that grow in tidal areas along coastlines. Mangrove forests are important for protecting coastlines from erosion and storm surges and also provide habitat for marine life.

Moss Forests

These are forests that are characterized by a dense layer of moss on the ground and trees. Often found in high-altitude areas, they host a unique biodiversity.
