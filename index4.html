Marine Resources

The Philippines is an archipelago nation in Southeast Asia, renowned for its rich marine resources. With over 7,000 islands and an extensive coastline, the country boasts a remarkable diversity of marine ecosystems, including:

Coral Reefs

The Philippines is part of the Coral Triangle. The country's coral reefs are home to a stunning array of fish, corals, invertebrates, and other marine life.

Seagrass Beds

Seagrass beds play a crucial role in marine ecosystems. They also help to stabilize coastlines and protect against erosion.

Estuaries

Estuaries are transitional zones between rivers and oceans. These unique habitats support a wide range of marine species, including fish, crustaceans, and shellfish.

Open Waters

The Philippines' open waters, including the South China Sea, Sulu Sea, and Philippine Sea, are home to a vast array of marine life, including pelagic fish, whales, dolphins, and sharks.
